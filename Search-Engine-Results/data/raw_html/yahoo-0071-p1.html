    What was the colossus of rhodes a statue of

    The Colossus of Rhodes was a statue of the sun god Helios. Erected on the Greek island of Rhodes around 280 BC, it was one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Standing approximately 33 meters (108 feet) tall, it symbolized the unity and strength of the people of Rhodes after their victory over Cyprus.

    Key Features

    • Material: Made primarily of bronze and iron.
    • Location: Positioned at the entrance of the harbor of Rhodes.
    • Destruction: The statue stood for about 56 years before being toppled by an earthquake in 226 BC.

    Cultural Significance
    The Colossus represented not only artistic achievement but also the resilience and pride of the Rhodians, becoming a lasting symbol of ancient engineering and artistry.
